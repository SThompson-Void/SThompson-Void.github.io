About Me

Hi, I'm Stephen Thompson, a driven and passionate Software Development Engineer and Computer Science major at San Francisco State University, graduating in 2027. With a solid foundation in programming and a knack for problem-solving, I thrive on creating innovative software solutions that make an impact.

I recently interned at Amazon AWS, where I contributed to the development of tools and workflows to optimize deployment processes. From designing developer bots to automating error handling and CI/CD tasks, I gained hands-on experience in large-scale codebases and cloud technologies.

I'm also the founder of Null Noir Software, where I develop and maintain a suite of tools tailored for efficient automation and seamless integration. My personal projects include building utility applications for the game Ark Survival Evolved, showcasing my creativity and technical versatility.

With expertise in C++, Java, Python, and AWS, as well as a strong foundation in data structures and algorithms, I am committed to learning, adapting, and collaborating to solve complex challenges. When I'm not coding, you'll find me exploring new technologies, optimizing workflows, or mentoring peers.

Feel free to check out my work and reach out—I’m always excited to connect and collaborate on meaningful projects!
